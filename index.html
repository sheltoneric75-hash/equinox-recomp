PHASE 1 (WEEKS 1–4)
Add 2.5–5 lbs to compound lifts each week. Add 1 rep to accessory movements. Maintain form strictly — do not sacrifice technique for weight.
PHASE 2 (WEEKS 5–8)
Wave loading: Week 5 moderate, Week 6 heavier, Week 7 heaviest, Week 8 back-off. Add drop sets and supersets to increase total volume without extra time.
PHASE 3 (WEEKS 9–12)
Max effort weeks 9–11. Week 12: PR attempts on squat, deadlift, weighted pull-up, and benchmark WOD. Log all numbers — these become your Phase 2 baseline next cycle.
Zone 2 Cardio
3× per week · 30–45 min · Fasted (morning preferred)
Assault bike or row erg at 60–65% max HR. Primary fat oxidation tool. Never perform directly after heavy lifting — keep a 4+ hour gap or do it in the AM before evening training.
HIIT / Metabolic Finishers
Embedded in 3 lifting days · 10–20 min post-lift
Keeps heart rate elevated post-training; amplifies EPOC for 24–36 hours. Finishers are already written into each workout day. Do not skip them — this is where fat comes off.
LISS Recovery Walk
Saturday or Sunday · 30–60 min
Brisk outdoor walk. Enhances mitochondrial density, improves recovery, burns additional calories without cortisol spike. Non-negotiable for longevity goals.
TIMING: End of Week 4 and End of Week 8
  • Reduce all working sets by 40–50% of normal load
  • Keep same exercises and movement patterns — do not change the program
  • Reduce intensity to RPE 5–6 (never grinding or hitting failure)
  • Prioritize mobility: add 20–30 min extra each deload session
  • Sleep 8–9 hours; optimize nutrition — this is when supercompensation occurs
  • Active recovery: daily 30 min Zone 2 walk
CALORIC TARGET

CARB CYCLING

DIETARY FATS
70–90g/day · Prioritize omega-3s and monounsaturated fats
MEAL TIMING
Pre-workout: carbs + protein 60–90 min before · Post-workout: fast protein within 30 min
⚠ Recomposition requires a slight caloric deficit combined with high protein. At 190 lbs and advanced training level, target 0.5–1 lb of weight loss per week while maintaining or slightly increasing lean muscle. Track weekly average weight — not daily fluctuations.
SLEEP
8–9 hours non-negotiable. Growth hormone peaks in deep sleep. This is when muscle is built and fat is mobilized. Compromise sleep, compromise the entire program.
DAILY MOBILITY MINIMUM
10 min every morning: hip 90/90, thoracic rotation, ankle circles, shoulder CARs. Do this before coffee, not as an afterthought.
SOFT TISSUE WORK
Foam roll 10 min, 3× per week minimum. Lacrosse ball for upper traps, glutes, pec minor. Reduces injury risk and accelerates recovery significantly.
SAUNA / COLD PROTOCOL
If available: 15–20 min sauna post-training 2–3× per week. Cold shower or plunge 2 min after. Reduces inflammation, accelerates recovery, improves sleep quality.
NORDIC CURLS + TURKISH GET-UPS
These two movements are non-negotiable for longevity. Nordic = bulletproof hamstrings against tears. TGU = total body coordination, shoulder stability, and hip mobility in one movement.
HEART RATE MONITORING
Use a HR monitor during conditioning. Zone 2 (60–65% max HR) for fat burning and recovery. Never let conditioning drift to Zone 4–5 unless it's intentional HIIT. Know the difference.
